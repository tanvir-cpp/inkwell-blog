# Digital Nomad Guide to Southeast Asia

Southeast Asia has long been a haven for digital nomads, and for good reason. The combination of low cost of living, incredible food, warm weather, and increasingly excellent internet infrastructure makes it one of the best regions on earth to work remotely.

## Top Cities for Digital Nomads

### 🇹🇭 Chiang Mai, Thailand

The OG digital nomad hub. Chiang Mai offers:
- **Cost of living:** $800–1,200/month
- **Internet:** Reliable fiber, great coworking spaces
- **Highlights:** Incredible street food, temples, mountain treks
- **Best for:** First-time nomads, long-term stays

### 🇻🇳 Da Nang, Vietnam

A rising star in the nomad community:
- **Cost of living:** $700–1,000/month
- **Internet:** Fast and reliable
- **Highlights:** Beautiful beaches, amazing pho, friendly locals
- **Best for:** Beach lovers, surfers, budget-conscious nomads

### 🇮🇩 Canggu, Bali

The Instagram-famous nomad paradise:
- **Cost of living:** $1,000–1,800/month
- **Internet:** Improving rapidly
- **Highlights:** Rice paddies, surf, wellness culture
- **Best for:** Creative professionals, wellness enthusiasts

### 🇲🇾 Kuala Lumpur, Malaysia

An underrated gem:
- **Cost of living:** $900–1,400/month
- **Internet:** Excellent infrastructure
- **Highlights:** World-class food, modern city, diverse culture
- **Best for:** City lovers, foodies, those wanting modern amenities

## Essential Tips

1. **Visa Research:** Always check current visa requirements. Many countries now offer digital nomad visas.
2. **Get a Local SIM:** Data plans are incredibly affordable in SEA.
3. **Coworking Spaces:** Join one! They're great for networking and reliable internet.
4. **Health Insurance:** Get proper coverage. SafetyWing and World Nomads are popular choices.
5. **Time Zones:** SEA (UTC+7/+8) works well with European clients and early morning calls with US teams.

## Packing Essentials

- A reliable laptop with good battery life
- Universal power adapter
- Noise-cancelling headphones
- A good water bottle (stay hydrated!)
- Rain jacket (tropical storms are real)

Southeast Asia isn't just a destination — it's a lifestyle. Whether you stay for a month or a year, these cities will inspire your best work and your biggest adventures.
